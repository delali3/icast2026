Background & Introduction

About the IEEE ICAST Conference

The IEEE International Conference on Adaptive Science & Technology is a double blind and an annually organised conference in the areas of Information Technology and Computer Science: Communication, Networking and Broadcast Technologies, Components, Circuits, Devices and Systems, Computing and Processing, Fields, Waves and Electromagnetics, Power, Energy and Industry Applications, Robotics and Control Systems. The proceedings of ICAST conferences are indexed in IEEE Xplore and Scopus etc.

Paper Submission

Guidelines and requirements for submitting your research

Author Guidelines

The IEEE reserves the right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference if the paper is not presented at the conference.

Papers are reviewed on the basis that they do not contain plagiarised material and have not been submitted to any other conference or periodicals at the same time.

IEEE Resources:

Plagiarism Guidelines
Double Submission Guidelines

Paper Format

All submissions should be written in English.

Full Papers:

Minimum 5 pages, Maximum 8 pages (including figures)

Short Papers:

Minimum 3 pages, Maximum 4 pages

Papers should adhere to the standard IEEE Conference Proceedings format.

Templates:

Microsoft Word & LaTeX Templates

Only PDF files and Word/ODT documents will be accepted for the review process.

Important Notice

Publication Requirements:

To be published in the ICAST 2026 Conference Proceedings and IEEE Xplore, an author of an accepted paper must:

  • Register for the conference
  • Present the paper at the conference
  • Pay non-refundable registration fees
  • Upload the final IEEE formatted paper

One registration is valid for up to 3 papers. Accepted and presented papers will be published in IEEE Xplore.
